The Passé Simple in French

The Passé Simple is a literary past tense in French, used primarily in formal writing, historical texts, and literature.

Formation of the Passé Simple

The passé simple is formed using specific endings added to the verb stem. The endings depend on the verb group (-ER, -IR, -RE) and can be quite different from other tenses.

Regular Verbs in the Passé Simple

Here are the endings and examples for regular verbs:

  • -ER verbs: parler → je parlai, tu parlas, il/elle parla...
  • -IR verbs: finir → je finis, tu finis, il/elle finit...
  • -RE verbs: vendre → je vendis, tu vendis, il/elle vendit...

1. Regular Verb Conjugations

While rarely used in conversation, the passé simple is important for reading French literature and historical texts.
