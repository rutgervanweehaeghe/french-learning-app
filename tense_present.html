The Present Tense in French

The Present Tense in French (le présent) is used to describe actions that are currently happening or general truths.

Formation of the Present Tense

The present tense is formed by taking the verb stem and adding the appropriate endings for each subject pronoun. Regular verbs follow predictable patterns.

Regular Verbs in the Present Tense

Here are the endings for regular verbs:

  • -ER verbs: parler → je parle, tu parles, il/elle parle...
  • -IR verbs: finir → je finis, tu finis, il/elle finit...
  • -RE verbs: vendre → je vends, tu vends, il/elle vend...

1. Regular Verbs

Here’s how regular verbs look in the present tense:

Subject Parler (to speak) Translation Finir (to finish) Translation Vendre (to sell) Translation
Je Je parle I speak Je finis I finish Je vends I sell
Tu Tu parles You speak Tu finis You finish Tu vends You sell
Il/Elle/On Il/Elle parle He/She/One speaks Il/Elle finit He/She/One finishes Il/Elle vend He/She/One sells
Nous Nous parlons We speak Nous finissons We finish Nous vendons We sell
Vous Vous parlez You speak Vous finissez You finish Vous vendez You sell
Ils/Elles Ils/Elles parlent They speak Ils/Elles finissent They finish Ils/Elles vendent They sell

Common Irregular Verbs

Some verbs are irregular and do not follow standard patterns:

  • Avoir (to have): j'ai, tu as, il/elle a, nous avons, vous avez, ils/elles ont
  • Être (to be): je suis, tu es, il/elle est, nous sommes, vous êtes, ils/elles sont
  • Aller (to go): je vais, tu vas, il/elle va, nous allons, vous allez, ils/elles vont
  • Faire (to do/make): je fais, tu fais, il/elle fait, nous faisons, vous faites, ils/elles font
  • Venir (to come): je viens, tu viens, il/elle vient, nous venons, vous venez, ils/elles viennent

The present tense is essential in everyday French and is often the first tense learners master.
